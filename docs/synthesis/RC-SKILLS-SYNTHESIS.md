## 5. Adversarial Validation: Three Passes

### Pass 1: Coverage Gaps

---

### Pass 2: Redundancy and Overlap Analysis

**Redundancy Assessment:**

- RC-S-001 (gap assessment) vs RC-S-017 (risk assessment): Distinct. Gap assessment is gap identification; risk assessment is vulnerability/impact analysis.
- RC-S-002 (interpretation) vs RC-S-014 (policy integration): Related but distinct. Interpretation is understanding; integration is organizational implementation.
- RC-S-004 (DPIA) vs RC-S-017 (risk assessment): Overlapping domain. DPIA is data-processing-specific; risk assessment is broader for compliance programs.
- RC-S-018 (requirements) vs RC-S-020 (design review): Sequential but distinct. Requirements gathering vs. design evaluation.
- RC-S-009 (training design) vs RC-S-015 (needs assessment): Sequential in training lifecycle. Assessment precedes design.

**Overlap with Framework Evidence:**
- Multiple high-STS elements map to multiple entries (as expected). No entry is redundant.

**Verdict:** All 22 entries are conceptually distinct. Strategic clustering of related elements across entries creates coherent skill progression.
