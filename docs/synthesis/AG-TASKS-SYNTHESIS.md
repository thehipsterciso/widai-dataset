## Concept Clusters

23 distinct task concept clusters extracted and validated from framework evidence:

### 1. Define and Maintain Enterprise AI Governance Framework
Establish enterprise-wide governance structures, policies, risk tiering criteria, review processes, and decommissioning standards (DCWF 5862, 5882; NIST RMF AIRMF-GV-1.6; EUAIA EUAIA-O-049)

### 2. Establish and Maintain AI System Inventory  
Create and manage enterprise AI system catalogs with metadata, risk tiers, owners, training data provenance, and model versions (NIST RMF AIRMF-GV-1.6; DCWF 5836; NICE S0820, T1792)

### 3. Lead and Conduct AI Ethics and Impact Reviews
Execute ethics reviews for high-stakes AI applications including bias assessment, fairness evaluation, and fundamental rights analysis (NIST RMF AIRMF-MS-2.11; EUAIA EUAIA-O-032; DCWF 5900, 7056)

### 4. Define Enterprise AI Acceptable Use Policy and Standards
Establish AI acceptable use policy, prohibited use categories, human oversight requirements, and ethical guidelines (DCWF 5893, 5863; NICE T0068; EUAIA EUAIA-C-008)

### 5. Conduct Iterative AI Risk Assessment Across Lifecycle
Perform multi-stage AI risk assessment at inception, development, deployment, and post-deployment with re-assessment triggers (NIST RMF AIRMF-MP-1.5; DCWF 5905, 5904; EUAIA EUAIA-O-002)

### 6. Manage Ongoing AI Policy Compliance and Monitoring
Conduct periodic review of deployed AI systems against policy, regulatory, and governance requirements (DCWF 1032, 1016, 5794; NICE T1549)

### 7. Execute AI Bias Audits and Fairness Assessment
Conduct systematic bias audits with documented methodology, findings, and recommended interventions (NIST RMF AIRMF-MS-2.11; DCWF 5873, 7056, 7041)

### 8. Develop and Maintain AI Assessment Tools, Templates, and Rubrics
Create assessment frameworks, evaluation rubrics, templates, and checklists for AI governance (DCWF 3734, 2304; NICE S0393, T1138)

### 9. Monitor AI Regulatory Developments and Assess Compliance Implications
Track emerging AI regulations and assess applicability to enterprise AI systems (NIST RMF AIRMF-GV-1.1; DCWF 1070A, 6912, 6913)

### 10. Operate AI Governance Review Processes
Manage governance intake, triage, stakeholder coordination, assessment scheduling, and executive escalation (NICE T1528, T1309, T1798; DCWF 5851)

### 11. Conduct Comprehensive AI Risk Assessment Across System Lifecycle
Perform systematic risk identification at each lifecycle stage: inception, development, deployment readiness, and post-deployment (EUAIA EUAIA-O-002, EUAIA-O-046; DCWF 5858, 5845)

### 12. Produce Regulatory-Grade AI System Documentation
Create technical specifications, risk records, conformity evidence, instructions-for-use, and regulatory compliance artifacts (EUAIA EUAIA-O-041, EUAIA-O-009, EUAIA-O-013; DCWF 8156; NICE T1422, T1529)

### 13. Design and Operate AI Post-Market Monitoring Systems
Establish continuous performance surveillance, risk signal identification, deployer data integration, and corrective action pathways (EUAIA EUAIA-O-049, EUAIA-O-050, EUAIA-O-029; NIST RMF AIRMF-MS-2.4, AIRMF-MS-3.1)

### 14. Manage AI System Incidents and Regulatory Reporting
Identify serious incidents, conduct root cause analysis, implement corrective actions, and execute regulatory reporting (EUAIA EUAIA-O-052, EUAIA-O-047; NIST RMF AIRMF-MG-1.4; DCWF 2840, 478)

### 15. Assess and Monitor Third-Party AI Risks
Evaluate third-party AI components, model provenance, vendor contracts, dependency tracking, and service disruption contingency (NIST RMF AIRMF-GV-6.1, AIRMF-MG-3.1; DCWF 5778; NICE T1892, T1893)

### 16. Govern AI Testing, Evaluation, Verification, and Validation
Establish TEVV governance including test protocol approval, methodology assessment, and process effectiveness evaluation (NIST RMF AIRMF-MS-2.13, AIRMF-MP-3.4; DCWF 5934, 5866; EUAIA EUAIA-O-004)

### 17. Conduct Fundamental Rights Impact Assessments
Execute impact assessments focused on fundamental rights, including rights identification and disproportionate impact analysis (EUAIA EUAIA-O-032; NIST RMF AIRMF-MP-3.1, AIRMF-MP-3.2; DCWF 5873)

### 18. Design and Deliver AI Governance Literacy and Training Programs
Develop competency assessments, role-based curricula, deliver governance training, and measure effectiveness (DCWF 2304, 2300, 2306; NICE T1420, T1415, T1421)

### 19. Establish and Operate Stakeholder Engagement and Feedback Mechanisms
Create feedback collection mechanisms for affected communities and integrate external perspectives into governance decisions (NIST RMF AIRMF-GV-5.1; EUAIA EUAIA-O-030; DCWF 5909, 5851)

### 20. Execute AI Conformity Assessment and Certification Procedures
Manage conformity assessment pathways, notified body engagement, CE marking, and post-assessment surveillance (EUAIA EUAIA-O-018, EUAIA-O-022, EUAIA-O-019; DCWF 516)

### 21. Implement AI Transparency and Disclosure Requirements
Design transparency mechanisms including interaction notification, synthetic content identification, and explainability documentation (EUAIA EUAIA-O-012, EUAIA-O-013; EUAIA-C-008; DCWF 5889)

### 22. Design and Maintain AI Risk Quantification and Measurement Approaches
Establish risk metrics, proxy indicators for unmeasurable risks, fairness measurement, and translate results into governance actions (NIST RMF AIRMF-MS-1.1, AIRMF-MS-1.2; DCWF 5873; NICE T1155)

### 23. Govern AI System Decommissioning
Plan safe phase-out procedures, assess transition risks, make data retention decisions, notify stakeholders, document rationale (NIST RMF AIRMF-GV-1.7, AIRMF-MG-1.3; DCWF 790; NICE T1512, T1186)
