## Adversarial Pass Results

### Pass 1 — Coverage Gaps

Reviewed all 300 WIDAI-relevant elements at STS ≥ 0.55 for concepts not captured by the 18 entries.

**Potential gaps examined:**

1. **Data and AI quality assurance processes** (DCWF 5926 at 0.6401, NICE T1956 at 0.6191): Evaluating AI performance, conducting monitoring assessments. Covered by LS-T-015 (AI risk assessment and monitoring infrastructure). Quality assurance is integral to the monitoring/risk infrastructure, not a separate task. Not added.

2. **Continuous analytical capability development** (DCWF 5907 at 0.6049): Planning and executing complex analytical studies. This overlaps with LS-T-004 (requirements translation) and LS-T-014 (architecture design). The task is subsumed in technical execution, not leadership-scoped. Not added.

3. **Intelligence analysis and operational support** (DCWF 2754 at 0.6989, NICE T1798 at 0.6324): Providing intelligence analysis to planning. This evidence is mapped across LS-T-002 through LS-T-008. It describes execution support, not leadership strategy. Not added as separate entry.

4. **Data collection and curation governance** (DCWF 7014 at 0.6463): Data acquisition best practices. This is Data Governance domain (DG), not Leadership & Strategy. Excluded on domain boundary grounds.

5. **Technology and tools development** (DCWF 5872 at 0.6931, 5871 at 0.6342): Designing AI tools and developing ML models. These are technical execution tasks that belong in AI/Analytics domain, not LS-T leadership scope. The evidence for LS-T-002/LS-T-003 is about leading teams who do this work, not executing it. Not added.

6. **Integration and data pipeline management** (DCWF 5852 at 0.6104, T0460 at 0.6163): Building data conduits and warehousing programs. These are technical data architecture execution tasks (DA domain), not leadership strategy. Not added.

**No new entries added beyond the 18 identified.**

## Methodology

Built from STRM rationale evidence per KSA-SYNTHESIS-METHODOLOGY.md v2.0.0. Prior entries were discarded; no prior entry text was used as a validation baseline. Concepts extracted from framework element descriptions across 6 frameworks, clustered into 12 independent concept groups based on semantic theme analysis of elements at STS ≥ 0.55, and written as task entries. All 18 entries were then subjected to 3 adversarial passes (coverage gaps, redundancy, domain boundary). No gaps identified, no merges required, no entries removed. Final count: 18, representing 7 more entries than the original 11 due to new distinct concept clusters identified in the evidence with STS ≥ 0.55 across multiple frameworks.

Entry count increase justification: The evidence shows sufficient framework corroboration (4–6 frameworks per entry, multiple elements at STS ≥ 0.55) for 7 additional leadership task concepts not explicitly represented in the original 11-entry set: AI adoption strategy design, adoption and culture change leadership, enterprise data architecture design, AI risk and compliance operations, value demonstration to executive leadership, strategic partnership coordination, and AI risk management training design. Each represents a distinct leadership task with independent framework evidence at high cross-framework density.
