## Gap Analysis

Initial review of 390 WIDAI-relevant elements at STS ≥ 0.55 identified that RC-T-013 (consent lifecycle) was attracting 1108 total mappings despite being semantically limited to user consent choices. Density analysis revealed 138 elements at STS ≥ 0.60, concentrated in testing (14 elements), monitoring (21 elements), and intelligence/collection requirements (8-10 elements at high STS).

**Gap identified: Collection Requirements Management.** Framework elements consistently describe managing organizational collection requirements, coordinating collection sources, modifying requirements based on operational needs, and evaluating collection effectiveness—concepts orthogonal to consent management. These 20 elements at STS ≥ 0.55 across DCWF, NICE, and DDaT form a coherent concept cluster requiring separate entry.

**New entry added (RC-T-014).** Expansion from 13 to 14 entries better captures distinct concepts supported by framework evidence while maintaining conceptual coherence within each entry. RC-T-013 is now properly scoped to user consent; RC-T-014 captures organizational collection strategy.

## Methodology

Built from STRM rationale evidence per KSA-SYNTHESIS-METHODOLOGY.md v2.0.0. All 13 existing entries were re-evaluated from first principles against framework element descriptions at STS ≥ 0.55. Adversarial density analysis revealed that RC-T-013 (1108 mappings) was capturing two semantically distinct concept clusters: user consent lifecycle and organizational collection requirements management. These were separated into RC-T-013 and RC-T-014, with RC-T-014 grounded in 20 distinct framework elements at STS ≥ 0.55 across DCWF and NICE. Concepts extracted from framework element descriptions, analyzed for redundancy and domain boundary, and mapped to RC task categories. All 14 entries were subjected to 3 adversarial passes. Final count: 14, representing complete and non-redundant coverage of the RC task concept space in WIDAI.

Final count justification: The expansion from 13 to 14 entries (addition of 1) reflects evidence indicating that while the original 13 entries capture major RC task categories, collection requirements management represents a distinct concept cluster with independent cross-framework corroboration (20 elements at STS ≥ 0.55, 8-10 at STS ≥ 0.60 in DCWF and NICE). This separation improves conceptual coherence: RC-T-013 now focuses exclusively on user consent (individual privacy), while RC-T-014 addresses organizational collection strategy and coordination (operational compliance). The 14-entry structure provides comprehensive coverage without over-fragmentation.
