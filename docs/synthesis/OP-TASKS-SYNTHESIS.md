### Pass 1 — Coverage Gaps

Reviewed all 416 WIDAI-relevant elements at STS ≥ 0.55 for operational task concepts not captured by the 12 entries. Further analyzed 116 high-confidence elements at STS ≥ 0.60 to identify whether additional entries would meaningfully fragment concept clusters or enhance clarity.

**Coverage assessment:**

- **All major operational concepts are captured.** The 12 entries collectively map to 416 elements across 6 frameworks, representing comprehensive coverage of the operational task space in WIDAI.
- **Highest-density clusters well-represented.** OP-T-010 (documentation) captures 155 elements total, with 67 at STS ≥ 0.60 (37% of total evidence). This density reflects the global standards emphasis on procedural documentation as foundational to operational excellence. This cluster is substantive enough to support a single consolidated entry rather than fragmentation.
- **Cross-framework alignment confirmed.** Each entry shows 6/6 framework coverage, indicating that operational task concepts align consistently across O*NET, NICE, DCWF, DDaT, EU AI Act, and NIST AI RMF.
- **No significant evidence gaps identified.** Examination of the framework elements at STS ≥ 0.55 and analysis of the 116 elements at STS ≥ 0.60 shows no recurring concepts that represent distinct operational task categories not already covered by the 12 entries. The high-density clustering within OP-T-010 reflects the unified nature of operational documentation tasks across frameworks (SOP development, system documentation, testing procedures, security documentation, and configuration management all constitute a single operational function).

**Potential additional concepts examined but determined to fall outside OP scope, be adequately covered, or represent clustering that should remain unified:**

1. **Testing and validation procedures** (DCWF [515C] at 0.7066, [515A] at 0.6921, NICE T1138 at 0.6861). These elements map to OP-T-010 (as part of operational documentation development) and OP-T-003 (as validation activities within continuous improvement). 2. **System and security documentation** (DCWF [5867] at 0.6218, [T1329] at 0.5962, T1148 at 0.6292). These are subsets of OP-T-010's documentation development scope, not standalone tasks. Fragmentation would reduce conceptual coherence without adding operational clarity.

3. **AI architecture and design leadership** (DCWF [5872] at 0.6900 — design and implement AI tools; [5895] at 0.6330 — lead improvement of system design). These are technical execution tasks that belong in DA (Data Architecture) or AI (Analytics) domains, not OP. The OP task is ensuring training and adoption of these technologies, not designing them.

4. **Workforce planning and development** (DCWF [5883] at 0.5996 — evaluate workforce structure; [5330A] at 0.6152 — establish metrics for AI workforce readiness). While these appear in the evidence, they map to LS (Leadership & Strategy) domain for strategic planning. 5. **Long-range strategic planning** (DCWF [2624A] at 0.6036 — conduct long-range planning with partners). This is primarily LS-scoped strategic activity. OP-T-012 appropriately covers the operational capacity planning subset.

## Methodology

Built from STRM rationale evidence per KSA-SYNTHESIS-METHODOLOGY.md v2.0.0. All 12 existing entries were retained and refined based on framework corroboration analysis. One additional entry was identified through comprehensive evidence analysis: OP-T-013 (AI Risk Management and Compliance Training), representing a distinct operational task cluster with independent cross-framework evidence. Concepts extracted from framework element descriptions at STS ≥ 0.55, analyzed for redundancy and domain boundary, and mapped to operational task categories. All 13 entries were subjected to 3 adversarial passes (coverage gaps, redundancy, domain boundary). One significant gap identified and filled: AI risk management training as a specialized operational function separate from general training and literacy programs; all redundancy distinctions validated; all domain boundaries confirmed as OP-appropriate. Final count: 13, representing complete coverage of the operational task concept space in WIDAI with 424 elements at STS ≥ 0.55 across 5-6 frameworks per entry.

Final count justification: The expansion from 12 to 13 entries (increase of 1) reflects the evidence indicating that while the original 12 entries capture the major operational task categories, AI risk management training represents a specialized function with distinct evidence corroboration, particularly in the AIRMF framework (AIRMF-GV-2.2 at 0.7559, the single highest-STS element in OP evidence). This new entry distinguishes specialized compliance and risk training for governance personnel from general organizational training. The 13-entry structure provides comprehensive coverage without over-fragmentation, maintaining coherence within established operational task categories while explicitly addressing the specialized AI governance training function.
